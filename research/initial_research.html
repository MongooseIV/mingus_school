Phonaesthetics: the study of the pleasantness of speech sounds.

Euphony: sounding pleasant

Cacophony: sounding unpleasant

Some sounds have been found to be considered more euphonic than others (such as /l, n, m, s, k, r, t, d/), others more cacophonous (such as /ð, ʒ, θ/). However, this data is likely by influenced by semantic connections.

(Crystal 1995)
